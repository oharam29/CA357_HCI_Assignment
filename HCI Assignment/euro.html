UEFA EURO 2020 qualifying draw

Gareth Southgate's England were handed a straightforward path to the 2020 European Championship at Sunday's qualification draw in Dublin. England will play Czech Republic, Bulgaria, Montenegro and new opponents Kosovo in Group A with the top two progressing to the finals that will be played across 12 host cities. Gareth Southgate's side also have an alternative route to Euro 2020 should they need it, having reached next summer's inaugural Nations League finals along with Holland, Portugal and Switzerland. Republic of Ireland can also have reason to be pleased with their draw, joining Switzerland, Denmark, Georgia and Gibraltar in Group C. There were audible cheers in the Dublin venue when Ireland were moved from Group B - which contains Germany and Holland - because of rules restricting the number of tournament host cities in any one group.
